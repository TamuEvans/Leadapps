Leadapps Personality & Career Navigator Questionnaire
Instructions for the Student:
"Welcome to the Leadapps Personality & Career Navigator! This questionnaire is designed to help you understand more about your preferences, interests, and work styles. There are no right or wrong answers – just choose the option that feels most like you, most of the time. Your honest responses will help us suggest study fields, careers, and university environments that might be a great fit for your unique profile. This is a tool for self-discovery, so relax and enjoy the process!"
Section 1: How You Interact and Get Energized
(Focus: Interaction Style)
(Format: Forced-choice or Likert scale. For this example, using forced-choice for brevity.)
When starting a new project, I usually prefer to:
A) Discuss ideas with a group to get initial energy and direction.
B) Think things through on my own first to develop my initial concepts.
After a busy week of social activities, I tend to feel:
A) Energized and ready for more.
B) Drained and in need of some quiet time to recharge.
In a learning environment, I learn best by:
A) Actively participating in discussions and group activities.
B) Listening, observing, and reflecting on the material presented.
When meeting new people, I am more likely to:
A) Easily strike up conversations with several people.
B) Prefer to have a deeper conversation with one or two individuals.
My ideal weekend often involves:
A) Spending time with a number of friends or engaging in group outings.
B) Having quiet time for my hobbies, reading, or spending time with a close friend or two.
(Scoring: Count A's for "Group-Energized" and B's for "Reflection-Energized")
Section 2: How You Perceive and Process Information
(Focus: Information Focus)
When learning a new skill, I prefer:
A) Clear, step-by-step instructions and practical examples.
B) Understanding the overall concept and how it connects to other ideas.
I am usually more interested in:
A) The factual details and specifics of a situation.
B) The underlying patterns, possibilities, and future implications.
When describing an event, I tend to focus on:
A) What actually happened, providing specific details in sequence.
B) The overall impression or meaning of the event and what it might lead to.
I trust information more if it is:
A) Based on direct experience, tangible evidence, and proven methods.
B) Innovative, thought-provoking, and opens up new perspectives.
In problem-solving, I first tend to:
A) Look at the current realities and practical constraints.
B) Brainstorm various possibilities and imaginative solutions.
(Scoring: Count A's for "Detail-Oriented & Practical" and B's for "Big-Picture & Conceptual")
Section 3: How You Make Decisions
(Focus: Decision Approach)
When making an important decision, I primarily rely on:
A) Logical analysis, objective criteria, and a clear assessment of pros and cons.
B) My personal values, how the decision will impact others, and what feels right.
I believe it's more important to be:
A) Fair and consistent, applying principles equally to everyone.
B) Compassionate and understanding, considering individual circumstances.
In a disagreement, I am more likely to focus on:
A) The logical flaws in an argument and finding the most rational solution.
B) Maintaining harmony and ensuring everyone feels heard and respected.
When giving feedback, I tend to be:
A) Direct, honest, and focused on areas for improvement, even if it's critical.
B) Tactful, encouraging, and focused on positive aspects and potential.
A good decision for me is one that:
A) Is demonstrably effective and based on sound reasoning.
B) Aligns with my core beliefs and contributes positively to relationships.
(Scoring: Count A's for "Logic-Driven & Objective" and B's for "Values-Driven & Empathetic")

(Focus: Work Organization)
I generally prefer to work on projects where:
A) There's a clear plan, deadlines are set, and tasks are well-defined.
B) There's flexibility to explore options, adapt as I go, and work in bursts of energy.
When it comes to deadlines, I usually:
A) Like to finish tasks well in advance to avoid last-minute pressure.
B) Find I do my best work closer to the deadline when the pressure is on.
My workspace tends to be:
A) Organized and tidy, with things in their designated places.
B) More flexible, with various projects and materials accessible as needed.
When planning a trip, I am more likely to:
A) Create a detailed itinerary and make reservations well in advance.
B) Have a general idea of where I want to go and decide on specifics as I travel.
I feel more comfortable when:
A) Decisions are made, and I know what to expect.
B) Options are kept open, and I can respond to new information or opportunities.
(Scoring: Count A's for "Planned & Structured" and B's for "Flexible & Spontaneous")

Output & Profile Generation:
Based on the responses, the Leadapps system would generate a profile summary:
Interaction Style: e.g., "Primarily Group-Energized"
Information Focus: e.g., "Leans towards Big-Picture & Conceptual"
Decision Approach: e.g., "Strongly Values-Driven & Empathetic"
Work Organization: e.g., "Prefers a Planned & Structured approach"
Top 3 Core Activity Interests: e.g., 1. Helping & Guiding Others, 2. Creating & Designing, 3. Organizing & Supporting.
Key Work Function/Environment Preferences: e.g., "Prefers collaborative tasks," "Values continuous learning," "Thrives in a moderately paced environment."
Top 5 Core Values: e.g., 1. Helping Others, 2. Learning & Growth, etc.
This profile is then used by the Leadapps matching engine to suggest:
Study Fields: e.g., Social Work, Education, Graphic Design, Event Management.
Career Paths: e.g., Teacher, Counsellor, UI/UX Designer, Non-profit Manager.
University/College Environment Types: e.g., "Smaller, community-focused universities," "Institutions known for strong arts programs and collaborative learning."
This questionnaire provides a framework. The number of questions in each section would need to be expanded for better reliability, and the wording carefully crafted and tested. Remember the legal and psychometric disclaimers!
